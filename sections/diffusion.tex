A fair comparison with Qwen won't be possible since Qwen 0.5B was a 500 million  parameter model as compared to 8 billion in LLaDA.
However, we can compare the performance over different number of denoising steps $T \in \{8, 16, 32, 64\}$ for the model, with no of tokens at each step to be unmasked linearly decreasing with no of steps.

Best performance observed at T = 32 (80\%). Format failures were relatively high at lower steps, likely because at each such step the model would need to unmask large number of tokens, including under-confident ones and stay with them. Therefore, it had less chances to correct its formatting. When I increased T to 64, its errors are not related to formatting but actual reasoning which get uncovered as an actual limitation.

\subsection*{Examples}
When $T \ge 32$, errors on complex Level 3 problems are mostly due to poor state-tracking rather than syntax errors. For example: \\
\texttt{glorp(flarn(snib(12, 4), 4))} $\rightarrow$ Expected: 12 | Got: 8. \\
The model computes the inner operation ($12-4=8$) but fails to propagate the result through the outer addition.
